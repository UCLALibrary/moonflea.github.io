Greetings, Friends & Colleagues
The annual meeting of the Archivists & Librarians in the History of the Health Sciences (ALHHS) is just around the corner! On Wednesday, April 6th and Thursday, April 7th we will be meeting in beautiful Birmingham, Alabama in conjunction with the American Association for the History of the Health Sciences (AAHM).
The annual dinner on Wednesday evening will be held at the elegant Tutwiler Hotel, which is located just 2.5 blocks from the main AAHM conference hotel. This year marks the thirtieth anniversary of the founding of the Archivists and Librarians in the History of the Health Sciences, and to recognize this milestone an anniversary program has been planned for the evening. Join us for a multi-media presentation, games and brain teasers, and an opportunity to reminisce about ALHHS meetings' past. Fun will be had by one and all, both new members and those who can actually remember the first meetings! The Medical Museums Association (MeMA) has been invited to join us for the evening. On Thursday buses will depart the Sheraton at 8:00 a.m. for UAB’s Lister Hill Library of the Health Sciences, where the ALHHS meeting will be held. After lunch and the business meeting, tours of the Alabama Museum of the Health Sciences and the Reynolds Historical Library will be conducted from 2:30 to 4:00 p.m.
For information on the Sheraton-Birmingham, the conference hotel, visit the hotel’s website at http://www.sheraton.com/Birmingham. One important item worth noting is that the Sheraton does offer a free shuttle for ground transportation between the airport and the hotel. We also encourage you to stay for AAHM; online registration is available at http://www.cme.uab.edu/aahm.
Vulcan Park, McWane Center, Birmingham Museum of Art, Ruffner Mountain Nature Center, and Birmingham Civil Rights Institute (site of this year’s Garrison Lecture reception) are just a few of the attractions that truly make this The Magic City. And we haven’t even mentioned the multitude of dining options found throughout the city, offering everything from soul food and barbecue to a traditional meat-and-three to nationally ranked French cuisine. A quick look at the Greater Birmingham Convention & Visitors Bureau website will, we think, convince you that “Magic” is not an inappropriate adjective for this burgeoning metropolis of the South.
With spring in full bloom and with help from Mother Nature, early April should be an absolutely delightful time to meet. Join your colleagues and renew old friendships in the heart of the South. Hope to see ya’ll soon!
