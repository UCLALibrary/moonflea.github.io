This Association is established exclusively for educational purposes to serve the professional interests of librarians, archivists, and other specialists actively engaged in the librarianship of the history of the health sciences by promoting the exchange of information and by improving standards of service.

 

News (11 May 2015)

  • The 2015 Program Committee invites ALHHS and MeMA members to take a brief survey on the program from the conference in New Haven a little more than a week ago. Please provide your feedback at: https://www.surveymonkey.com/s/LLZKCV6.   (Abstracted from ALHHS-L posting by Rachel Ingold, 5 May 2015)


  • ALHHS President Stephen Novak appointed Lisa Mix as the new chair of the Nominations Committee. Lisa and committee members Christopher Lyons and Russell Johnson recruited a slate of members to stand for election. Barbara Niss was unanimously approved at the annual business meeting to continue as Acting Treasurer, pending the results of the annual election. In approximately two weeks, all dues-paying members will receive an invitation to cast ballots for new officers (Vice President/President-elect, Treasurer, and two Members-at-Large) via Survey Monkey. If you are not sure you have paid your membership dues for 2015, please contact our secretary, Phoebe Evans Letocha (pletocha AT jhmi.edu). Voting will be open for only two weeks after the Survey Monkey invitations go out. (Abstracted from ALHHS-L posting by Stephen Novak, 7 May 2015


  • ALHHS Awards Committee chair Jim Edmonson presented three prizes at the 2015 annual meeting in New Haven:
    • Best Monograph Award (2015) -- Shelly McKenzie. Getting Physical: The Rise of Fitness Culture in America (Lawrence, Kansas: University of Kansas Press, 2013)
    • Best Article Award (2015) -- Jeffrey S. Reznick. “Embracing the Future as Stewards of the Past: Charting a Course Forward for Historical Medical Libraries and Archives,” RBM: A Journal of Rare Books, Manuscripts, and Cultural Heritage Autumn 2014, 15:111-123
    • Best Online Resource Award (2015) -- Michelle DiMeo (Conference Organizer, Co-Author, and Editor), for "Emerging Roles for Historical Medical Libraries: Value in the Digital Age," which appeared in open access format in RBM: A Journal of Rare Books, Manuscripts, and Cultural Heritage, Autumn 2014. URL: http://rbm.acrl.org/content/vol15/issue2/index.dtl
