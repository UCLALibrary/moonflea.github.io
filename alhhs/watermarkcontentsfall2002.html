A "Trinitarian Plan": The Historical Library, Cushing/Whitney Medical Library, Yale University.

Toby A. Appel, Historical Librarian at the Cushing/Whitney Medical Library, Yale University, traces the history of one of the finest historical medical collections in the country. The "Trinitarian Plan" combined the collections of three bibliophiles: Harvey Cushing, renowned neurosurgeon and historian of medicine; John Fulton, physiologist and professor at Yale University; and Arnold C. Klebs, specialist in the treatment of tuberculosis and compiler of the celebrated bibliography of medical incunabula.

From the President

President of ALHHS, Jodi Koste, describes the 66th Annual meeting of the Society of American Archivists. Of particular interest to ALHHS members was the session entitled "Assisting Researchers and Protecting Privacy: Managing Access to Patient Information under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act."

Ex Libris

Compiled and edited by Lucretia McClure, Ex Libris contains news and announcements from Medical Historical Collections. Featured in this issue is a description of several exhibits, including The Mount Sinai Hospital, 1852-2002, celebrating the sesquicentennial of the hospital; A Passage to Oblivion, the fate of Burt Asper, surgeon aboard the U.S.S. Cyclops, whose ship disappeared off Bermuda without a trace in early March 1918; and William Helfand's exhibit Quack, Quack, Quack: the Seller of Nostrums in Prints, Posters, Ephemera and Books on display at the Grolier Club through November 23. The issue also features the lecture schedules of the C.F. Reynolds Medical History Society at the University of Pittsburgh, the College of Physicians of Philadelphia, the University of Alabama, and the New York Academy of Medicine.

News from HMD

Elizabeth Fee, Chief of the History of Medicine Division at the National Library of Medicine, reports on the activities of the Division, including Dream Anatomy, a new exhibition of material drawn mainly from the historical collections at NLM from the early modern period to the present.
