Deborah Nadoolman Landis

Deborah Nadoolman Landis grew up in New York City and was awarded the first-ever grant for costume design from the National Endowment for the Arts. Since receiving her MFA in Costume Design from UCLA in 1975 she has worked in nearly every capacity in costume, both in the theater and in film. Her work appears in some of the most popular films and videos ever made: The Blues Brothers, Raiders of the Lost Ark, Michael Jackson's Thriller, and Coming to America, for which she received an Academy Award nomination.

Landis holds a doctorate in the History of Design from the Royal College of Art, London, and is currently president of the Costume Designers Guild. Following publication of her book Costume Design, she edited the Academy of Motion Picture Arts & Sciences catalogue 50 Costumes/50 Designers: Concept to Character. Landis is currently at work on A Century of Hollywood Costume, to be published in 2006. She is married to filmmaker John Landis.
