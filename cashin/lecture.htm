THE ROAD TO JOHN FANTE'S LOS ANGELES:
A Biographer Reflects


Although his major literary works languished for decades in out-of-print oblivion and his creative energies were sapped writing Hollywood "piffle," John Fante (1909-1983) has survived to enjoy a remarkable afterlife as the patron saint of Los Angeles writers and an influential figure in twentieth-century American letters.

Stephen Cooper will discuss his work on Fante, considering critical swerves in the writer's still developing reputation and the biographer's challenges in mapping the course.
