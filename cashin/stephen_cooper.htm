Cooper's LA Times article

THE BONNIE CASHIN ARCHIVE

 Stephen Cooper is professor of English at California State University, Long Beach (CSULB), where he teaches creative writing, literature, and film. His work on John Fante has helped revive the memory of a once-forgotten Los Angeles writer whose legacy, now central to the city's literary identity, is celebrated internationally.

In 1995, Cooper organized the first John Fante conference, which led to the 1999 publication of John Fante, A Critical Gathering. His investigations into the author's life and career included a summer at the Charles E. Young Research Library Department of Special Collections reading the diaries of Fante's best friend, Carey McWilliams. In his research, Cooper discovered and edited the unpublished writings that became Fante's last book, The Big Hunger: Stories 1932-1959, published in 2000, along with Cooper's Full of Life: A Biography of John Fante. Both the biography and his 2002 edition of The John Fante Reader were named among the Los Angeles Times Best Books of the Year.

For his work on Fante, Cooper received CSULB's Distinguished Faculty Scholarly and Creative Activities Award. He is also the recipient of a National Endowment for the Arts fellowship. A graduate of UCLA's Department of English, Cooper holds an MFA in creative writing from the University of California, Irvine, and a Ph.D in English from the University of Southern California. Among his other publications are Perspectives on John Huston and numerous short stories, essays, and articles on the interactions of literature, film, culture, and history.
