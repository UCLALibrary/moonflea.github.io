SYMPOSIUM PROGRAM
Friday, October 14, 2005
12:30 p.m. - 6:00 p.m.
Charles E. Young Library Conference Center
12:30 Registration, refreshments, getting acquainted
1:00 Welcoming remarks from the UCLA Library

Victoria Steele, Head, UCLA Charles E. Young Library Department of Special Collections
1:10

Becoming Paul Monette
Exhibit curator and conference organizer Dan Luckenbill introduces Paul Monette's authorized biographer Chris Freeman, who will present and interpret the panorama of Monette's life and work.
Speakers: Dan Luckenbill, Chris Freeman

1:30 Session 1: Friends and Writers Read and Discuss the Works of Paul Monette
Participants working in a variety of professions and writing genres discuss Monette's work, including text by Monette in relation to their presentations, followed by time for questions.
Moderator: Chris Freeman
Panel: Betty Berzon, Eric Gutierrez, Mark Thompson
2:45 Break
3:00

Session 2: Working With and From Paul Monette
An artist, composer, poet, and novelists - representatives of several generations - discuss their work as collaborators with or as inspired by the vision of Paul Monette. These participants will also read a brief text by Monette as part of their presentation. A time for questions will follow.
Moderator: Christopher Looby
Panel: Roger Bourland, David Groff, Terry Wolverton

4:00 Break
4:15 The UCLA Library Exhibit, One Man's Truth: The Life and Work of Paul Monette (1945-1995)
Speaker: Dan Luckenbill
4:30 The Horwitz Family, the Monette Family, and the Monette-Horwitz Trust
Representatives: Bernice Horwitz, Winston Wilde, Robert L. Monette
4:45 Remarks by Malcolm Boyd
5:00 Exhibit viewing and reception
Charles E. Young Research Library Department of Special Collections
A1713 Young Research Library
5:45 Closing remarks
Representative of the UCLA LGBT Faculty / Staff Network (reception sponsor)
Representative of The Monette-Horwitz Trust (as introduction to the Faculty Center dinner)
7:00-10:00 Dinner to present the Monette-Horwitz Trust Award and honor the life of Paul Monette.
